How the Math Works

Your horizon distance depends directly on your altitude above the ground. If Earth were a perfect sphere without atmosphere, the geometric distance to the horizon would be:

d_km = 3.57 * sqrt(h_m)

where h_m is your cruise altitude converted to meters.

However, because Earth's atmosphere refracts light downward, you can actually see slightly beyond the geometric horizon. With standard atmospheric refraction, the theoretical maximum visibility is:

d_km = 3.86 * sqrt(h_m)

In the real world, atmospheric haze, dust, and moisture will reduce what the human eye can pick out on any given day.
